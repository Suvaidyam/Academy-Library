    • Vission

      To establish IGGAARL as an economically self-reliant global centre of excellence focussing on a farmer-centric approach towards agroecological farming, agriculture-centric livelihoods, value-chains, and other relevant areas across the agroecology based natural farming.

    • Objectives

      The Indo German Global Academy for Agroecological Research and Learning (IGGAARL) has been established as a light house centre and knowledge hub in the field of Agroecology with an objective to provide scientific evidence and promote traditional and innovative Agroecological and Natural Farming practices.

    History

    The Indo-German Global Academy for Agroecology Research and Learning (IGGAARL) was established in 2022 in Pulivendula, Y.S.R. Kadapa, Andhra Pradesh. IGGAARL is an institution setup for research and learning on diverse themes of agroecology specifically focusing on farmer centric approach that is ‘of the farmers ,for the farmers, and by the farmers. In addition to the main campus, there are 19 district-level learning centers across Andhra Pradesh, catering to localized learning needs. These 19 sub-centres actively conduct learning activities to enhance agroecological education and training. Research and training activities take place across various hubs in the state, ensuring widespread impact and accessibility.

    Leadership

    CIGGAARL operates under the leadership of RySS (Rythu Sadhikara Samstha) in collaboration with the Government of Andhra Pradesh and the Government of Germany. RySS serves as the Project Executive Agent, overseeing research, training, and capacity-building initiatives. Main aim of IGGAARL is to co-create and share knowledge and practices, science and innovation to influence decision-making in agroecology. It is an institute of excellence of Natural Farming that will transform each village as a Climate Resilient Village. For this, IGGAARL facilitates empirical farmer-led research, promotes learning and innovation in the agroecology space, and act as a knowledge centre for upscaling and dissemination of the research findings and learning activities at the national as well as global level.

    Admissions for Natural Forming

    The Natural Forming program focuses on the study and application of natural material formation processes, including geological, metallurgical, and organic transformations. Students gain hands-on experience with natural shaping techniques, sustainable material use, and scientific analysis of natural forms. This program blends theory with practical learning to prepare individuals for careers in environmental science, materials engineering, or artisan crafts.

    • Focus on eco-friendly and sustainable forming processes.
    • Hands-on training in natural material manipulation.
    • Study of geological and environmental formation systems.
    • Interdisciplinary approach combining science, art, and engineering.
    • Opportunities for fieldwork and research projects.
    • Ideal for careers in materials science, geology, environmental design, or crafts.
