About Academy

The Indo-German Global Academy for Agroecology Research and Learning (IGGAARL) was established in 2022 in Pulivendula, Y.S.R. Kadapa, Andhra Pradesh. IGGAARL is an institution setup for research and learning on diverse themes of agroecology specifically focusing on farmer centric approach that is ‘of the farmers, for the farmers, and by the farmers.

In addition to the main campus, there are 19 district-level learning centres across Andhra Pradesh, catering to localized learning needs. These 19 sub-centres actively conduct learning activities to enhance agroecological education and training. Research and training activities take place across various hubs in the state, ensuring widespread impact and accessibility. IGGAARL operates under the leadership of RySS (Rythu Sadhikara Samstha) in collaboration with the Government of Andhra Pradesh and the Government of Germany. RySS serves as the Project Executive Agent, overseeing research, training, and capacity-building initiatives. Main aim of IGGAARL is to co-create and share knowledge and practices, science and innovation to influence decision-making in agroecology. It is an institute of excellence of Natural Farming that will transform each village as a Climate Resilient Village. For this, IGGAARL facilitates empirical farmer-led research, promotes learning and innovation in the agroecology space, and act as a knowledge centre for upscaling and dissemination of the research findings and learning activities at the national as well as global level.

Key Attributes

IGGAARL’s approach to agroecology research and learning is characterized by the following core attributes:

  1. Farmer-centric and farmer-driven research
  2. Establishment of agroecology standards, testing protocols, and research centres
  3. Provision of agroecology learning, knowledge-sharing, research, and consultancy services
  4. Development of both on-farm and off-farm laboratories for primary research
  5. Focus on diverse research areas including:
    1. Carbon sequestration
    2. Socio-economic aspects of agroecology
    3. Pest management
    4. Biogeochemical cycles
    5. Value chains and market linkages
    6. Economics and sustainability of agroecology
  6. Creation of a digital repository for data and information
  7. Publication of research in peer-reviewed journals of international prominence
  8. Establishment of global knowledge platforms for knowledge exchange

      The IGGAARL’s vision focuses on creating a thriving community of Farmer Scientists, generating scientific evidence and documented knowledge on agroecological models, promoting agroecology, and fostering collaborations to drive positive change in agriculture both nationally and globally. Through its dedication to research, knowledge exchange, and sustainable practices, IGGAARL seeks to be at the forefront of the global movement towards an ecologically balanced and resilient agricultural system. Climate resilience will be an overarching theme across various activities of IGGAARL, including the research and learning activities. The outcomes of IGGAARL’s initiatives are expected to be significant and far- reaching, with the generation of tremendous amounts of research findings in natural farming. This research will contribute to support evidence-based agricultural policies, support farmers in adopting sustainable practices, and address climate change challenges in the agricultural sector.
