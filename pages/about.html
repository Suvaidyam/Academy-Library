About IGGAARL

About

Indo-German Global Academy for Agroecology Research and Learning (IGGAARL) is a collaborative initiative between the Government of Andhra Pradesh and the Government of Germany, aimed at promoting agroecological research and learning. The academy serves as a global hub for knowledge exchange, capacity building, and innovation in agroecology, with a focus on sustainable agricultural practices and climate resilience.

IGGAARL is a pioneering initiative that aims to establish a global centre of excellence in agroecology research and learning. The academy is dedicated to advancing the principles of agroecology, which emphasizes sustainable farming practices, biodiversity conservation, and climate resilience. By fostering collaboration between farmers, researchers, and policymakers, IGGAARL seeks to drive positive change in agriculture and promote sustainable livelihoods.

IGGAARL operates under the aegis of Rythu Sadhikara Samstha (RySS) in collaboration with the Government of Andhra Pradesh and the Government of Germany. RySS serves as the Project Executive Agent, overseeing research, training, and capacity-building initiatives. Main aim of IGGAARL is to co-create and share knowledge and practices, science and innovation to influence decision-making in agroecology. It is an institute of excellence of Natural Farming that will transform each village as a Climate Resilient Village. For this, IGGAARL facilitates empirical farmer-led research, promotes learning and innovation in the agroecology space, and act as a knowledge centre for upscaling and dissemination of the research findings and learning activities at the national as well as global level.

History

The Indo-German Global Academy for Agroecology Research and Learning (IGGAARL) was established in 2022 at Pulivendula, Y.S.R. Kadapa, Andhra Pradesh. IGGAARL is an institution setup for research and learning on diverse themes of agroecology specifically focusing on farmer centric approach that is of the farmers, for the farmers, and by the farmers.

In addition to the main campus, there are 11 district-level learning centres, catering to localized learning needs of the 26 districts across Andhra Pradesh. These 11 centres actively conduct learning activities to enhance agroecological education and training. Research and training activities take place across various hubs in the state, ensuring widespread impact and accessibility.

VISION

To establish IGGAARL as an economically self-reliant global centre of excellence, focussing on a farmer-centric approach towards agroecological farming, agriculture-centric livelihoods, value-chains, and other relevant areas across the agroecology based natural farming.

OBJECTIVES

The Indo German Global Academy for Agroecological Research and Learning (IGGAARL) has been established as a light house centre and knowledge hub in the field of Agroecology with an objective to provide scientific evidence and promote traditional and innovative Agroecological and Natural Farming practices.

Research Development and Innovation
The goal is to conduct applied empirical and strategic research on the mechanisms and effectiveness of agroecological approaches, building evidence to strengthen the emerging trend of agroecology and natural farming. This includes farmer-focused, participatory research through collaborations, with the aim of publishing scientific articles, journals, and other publications dedicated to agroecology and natural farming. Additionally, we aim to foster partnerships with national and international research organizations to advance agroecological research and innovation.

Academic Learning and Education
The objective is to develop and implement educational courses on agroecology-based natural farming across the entire farming value chain. We aim to establish a knowledge centre for agroecological approaches, focusing on their dissemination through education, training, and other relevant means. Additionally, we plan to create pedagogical approaches using model plots and exemplar landscapes to facilitate the customized adaptation of agroecological practices, emphasizing the use of locally available resources.

Knowledge Management and Upscaling
The aim is to connect with and contribute to a network of organizations in Andhra Pradesh, other states in India, and internationally, supporting research, knowledge management, and the dissemination of agroecological approaches and natural farming. We seek to foster a spirit of innovation, encourage the adaptation of traditional knowledge, and promote responsible risk-taking among farmers. Additionally, we aim to establish partnerships for knowledge exchange, training, and capacity building with national and international institutions, including universities and research centres.
