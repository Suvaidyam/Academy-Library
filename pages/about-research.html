Academy Research Collaborations

Welcome to IGGAARL - Indo-German Global Academy for Agroecology Research and Learning

The Indian Council of Agricultural Research (ICAR) Institutes, State Agricultural Universities and other national and International Institutes are actively engaged in various innovative projects aimed at promoting natural farming and enhancing climate resilience across different agricultural systems. Below is an overview of their initiatives:

ICAR Institutes Initiatives

  • Indian Institute of Pulses Research (IIPR): Focused on implementing natural farming practices in chickpea production systems across Ananthapur, Prakasam, and Palnadu districts.

  • Central Institute for Cotton Research (CICR): Conducting a comparative study on natural farming methods in rainfed cotton cultivation in Ananthapur, Kurnool, and Palnadu.

  • National Institute of Abiotic Stress Management (NIASM): Working to ensure livelihood and environmental security through natural farming practices in Maharashtra and Andhra Pradesh.

  • Indian Institute of Soil and Water Conservation (IISWC): Investigating the effects of natural farming practices on runoff, soil health, and nutrient losses across various rainfall zones in Andhra Pradesh.

  • Indian Institute of Maize Research (IIMR): Exploring the potential of natural farming for sustainable maize-based cropping systems in three to four districts of Andhra Pradesh.

  • Central Research Institute for Dryland Agriculture (CRIDA): Implementing natural farming strategies to revitalize soil health, enhance sustainable productivity, and improve climate resilience in two districts of Andhra Pradesh, with on-station trials at the Hayathnagar Research Station in Hyderabad.

State Agricultural Universities

  • Acharya N.G. Ranga Agricultural University (ANGRAU): Conducting a comparative study of 365 days green cover under the Andhra Pradesh Community Natural Farming (APCNF) model across different farming systems.

  • Dr. Y.S.R. Horticultural University (Dr.YSRHU): Comparing the APCNF natural farming model with conventional cultivation methods for horticultural crops such as banana and coconut.

National Institutes

  • All India Institute of Medical Sciences (AIIMS), Mangalagiri: Analyzing the nutrient content of crops grown under natural farming versus conventional methods, while also studying the impact on the nutritional status of women in reproductive age in Andhra Pradesh.

  • M.S. Swaminathan Research Foundation (MSSRF): Examining the impacts of agroecological farming practices on soil microbiome dynamics, nutrient properties, and crop productivity compared to conventional practices.

  • Core CarbonX: Investigating the impact of natural farming on energy and water savings within Andhra Pradesh.

International Collaborations

  • University of Reading (UoR): Researching biophysical processes and extension mechanisms related to the Andhra Pradesh Community Managed Natural Farming to support its broader application.

  • Potsdam Institute for Climate Impact Research (PIK): Conducting spatial assessments to evaluate the production potential and climate resilience of natural farming practices across selected states in India.

We are actively reviewing several competitive research proposals received from various esteemed institutions. These proposals encompass a range of themes focused on advancing our understanding of natural farming practices and their implications for sustainability
