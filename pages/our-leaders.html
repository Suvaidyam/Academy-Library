H.E. Mr. Nara Chandrababu Naidu

Chief Minister, Andhra Pradesh

H.E. Chandrababu Naidu is a visionary leader and a transformative figure in Indian politics, with over four decades of public service. Renowned for pioneering reforms in technology, infrastructure, and governance, he has positioned Andhra Pradesh as a global investment hub.

As Chief Minister, Mr. Naidu integrated technology into governance, streamlined decision-making, and advanced public service delivery. His initiatives in education have made the state a centre for human capital development, producing over 100,000 engineers annually.

A champion of rural development, he empowered women and alleviated poverty through self-help groups like DWCRA. His focus on inclusive growth, health, and employment has significantly uplifted farmers and marginalized communities. Globally acknowledged for his contributions, Mr. Naidu has received accolades like IT Indian of the Millennium and Business Person of the Year (The Economic Times). Oracle’s Profit magazine recognized him as one of the “hidden seven” leaders transforming the world through innovation.

Mr. Thallam Vijay Kumar, IAS. (Retd.)

Executive Vice-Chairman

As the Executive Vice Chairman­ of Rythu Sadhikara Samstha (RySS) and Advisor to the government of Andhra Pradesh on promoting natural farming, he has dedicated over 40 years to public service, primarily focusing on empowering rural communities.

A Physics honours graduate with an MBA from Delhi University, he joined the Indian Administrative Service in 1983, serving predominantly in tribal development, community mobilization, livelihoods enhancement and women's collectives.

Notably, as CEO of the Society for Elimination of Rural Poverty from the year 2000 to 2010, he empowered 11.5 million rural women through self-help groups. He furthered this mission as the first Mission Director of the National Rural Livelihoods Mission (NRLM), mobilising 100 million women nationwide. Since 2015, he has spearheaded the Andhra Pradesh Community-Managed Natural Farming (APCNF) initiative, enrolling over 800,000 farmers. Under his leadership APCNF won the 2024 Gulbenkian Prize for Humanity. A former Vice Chair of the UN Food Systems Summit Champions Network, he continues to advocate for natural farming as a member of the Advisory Council for Promotion of Natural Farming.

Mr. G. Muralidhar

Senior Advisor

Mr. G. Muralidhar, is a distinguished alumnus of the Institute of Rural Management Anand (IRMA) and NIT Warangal. He is a National Talent Scholar and Mathematical Olympian, has over 40 years of experience in poverty reduction, social entrepreneurship, and participatory governance.

He has led SHG movements, developed livelihood strategies, and collaborated with civil society, philanthropists, and governments to uplift marginalized communities.

Notably, as CEO of the Society for Elimination of Rural Poverty from the year 2000 to 2010, he empowered 11.5 million rural women through self-help groups. He furthered this mission as the first Mission Director of the National Rural Livelihoods Mission (NRLM), mobilising 100 million women nationwide. Since 2015, he has spearheaded the Andhra Pradesh Community-Managed Natural Farming (APCNF) initiative, enrolling over 800,000 farmers. Under his leadership APCNF won the 2024 Gulbenkian Prize for Humanity. A former Vice Chair of the UN Food Systems Summit Champions Network, he continues to advocate for natural farming as a member of the Advisory Council for Promotion of Natural Farming.

Mr. D. V. Raidu, IAS. (Retd.)

Advisor

D. V. Raidu, IAS (Retd.), is a senior official with expertise in soil science and extensive experience in agriculture. With 17 years dedicated to promoting Community-Managed Natural Farming.

He focuses on empowering women-led CBOs to create sustainable, agroecological livelihoods and tackle climate challenges.

A practising natural farmer in Telangana, he cultivates an 8-acre food forest with crops like oil palm, guava, and vegetables, aiming for certification and carbon credits. Currently, he advises on natural farming science for the Rythu Sadhikara Samstha (RySS) under the APCNF project.

Dr. K. S. Varaprasad

Director, Research - Rythu Sadhikara Samstha

Dr. K. S. Varaprasad, former Director, ICAR - Indian Institute of Oilseeds Research is currently engaged as Director, Research, RySS - IGGAARL; Advisor, Biosecurity and Bio-resources, APAARI, and Chair, Working Group on Seed Systems of Revitalizing Rainfed Areas Network, WASSAN & Odisha Millet Mission.

He did his M.Sc. and PhD at IARI, New Delhi and taught post graduates for about 6 years. He worked as Head, National Bureau of Plant Genetic Resources Regional Station, Hyderabad for over two and half decades facilitating Plant Quarantine and Plant Genetic Resource services to over 50 public, private and international institutions in Southeast Coastal India. He has over 126 research and review publications to his credit and received several awards for his leadership and contributions that include Dodla Raghava Reddy Memorial Award, Late Sri P. P. Singhal Memorial Industry Award, UNDP – India Biodiversity Award, Dr. G. I. D’Souza Memorial Award; Lifetime International Agricultural Research Award in 2024 by the Asian PGPR at WVC, Taiwan for his contributions to the agricultural sciences; and recently Lifetime Achievement in Global Agriculture Award at the 8th International Conference on Plant Growth-Promoting Rhizobacteria for Sustainable Agriculture, World Vegetable Centre, Taiwan.
